Dr. Georgios Papanikolaou

May 13, 1883 - February 19, 1962

Thanks to Dr. Georgios Papanikolaou, millions of women arround the world today are saving their lives by preventing cancer of the cervix.


  • 1883 - (13 May) Born in Kymi, Greece.
  • 1904 - He received his medical degree from the University of Athens.
  • 1910 - He received his PhD from the University of Munich, Germany.
  • 1913 - He emigrated to the U.S. in order to work in the department of Pathology of New York Hospital and the Department of Anatomy at the Cornell Medical College Cornell University.
  • 1928 - He first reported that uterine cancer could be diagnosed by means of a vaginal smear.

  • 1950 - Papanicolaou was the recipient of the Albert Lasker Award for Clinical Medical Research.
  • 1961 - He moved to Miami, Florida, to develop the Papanicolaou Cancer Research Institute at the University of Miami.
  • 1962 - Died 19 February 1962 (aged 78) Miami, Florida, US.
